Manage your wallet

From the initial setup, to sending and receiving, the Monero desktop wallet guides you through each step and encourages good practices to protect your privacy and security.

Create a new wallet or import your existing one

Setting up a new wallet correctly is one of the most important things. We take you step-by-step through all the important steps, whether it’s your first or your 100th wallet.

Send & receive with a few clicks

With a built-in address book and history page, the wallet makes it easy to transfer funds and stay on top of transactions.

Manage multiple accounts

Splitting funds into savings, spending, business and other categories helps to stay organized. The wallet allows you to comfortably create and manage accounts.

Private by default

With support for hardware wallets and running your own node, the wallet encourages best practices to protect your funds and limit exposure of your activity.

For casual use

Cryptocurrencies can be complex. The Monero GUI offers a simple mode, for those who just need the basic features.

Streamlined interface

Send, receive, and manage accounts. That’s enough for many use cases, and the wallet offers a way to keep things this simple

Private by default

Everything is designed to protect your financial privacy wherever possible. No need to configure anything or understand how it all works (although that’s always better).

Quick start

Choose bootstrap mode to quickly initalize your wallet while the full blockchain is downloaded in the background. A good mix of convenience and privacy.

For power use

All the features you expect from a desktop cryptocurrency wallet.

Mining

Mine with your desktop CPU with just one click, control how many threads you want to use.

Sign & verify

A convenient way to secuurely communicate with others.

Prove & check

All the tools you need to prove payments you made to others, and validate that others have made payments to you.

Shared RingDB

Track ring members across wallets for improved privacy.

For business

While the Monero GUI does not offer a full suite of tools for businesses, it does have some useful features.

Merchant page

A simple point-of-sale screen to present invoices to customers in a retail setting.

Subaddresses

Easily create as many subaddresses as you need to uniquely identify individual sales, while still securing privacy for you and your customers.

Built-in privacy

Hardware wallet support

For an extra level of security, you can keep your privacy keys on a hardware wallet. Monero GUI supports both Ledger and Trezor wallets.

Run your own full node

The Monero network becomes more stable with every person that runs their own full node. The wallet allows you to do this with no configuration.

Your keys, your coins

Monero GUI is non-custodial. Your privacy keys never leave your computer. This puts you in full control of your funds.

I2P & Tor support

(coming soon)

To help anonymize network traffic between the wallet and the Monero network, I2P and Tor will be natively added as features soon.

Black or white?

Monero does not compromise on privacy. It’s black and white, just like the wallet . Choose your favorite theme.
